MOON RITUALS

Align with lunar cycles. Plant seeds and release burdens.

üåô Intention

To honor the rhythm of waxing and waning‚Äîin the moon and in yourself. To set intentions when the moon is dark and release what no longer serves when she is full.

The moon reminds us: everything has seasons. Growth and rest. Building and releasing. Both are sacred.

üåë New Moon: Planting Seeds

What You'll Need

Moonstone, Clear Quartz, or Selenite
Essential oil: Jasmine, Sandalwood, or Frankincense
Candle OR alternative (white or silver | LED | water bowl | visualization)
Journal and pen
Quiet space where you can see the night sky (optional)

1. Create Sacred Space

Light your candle (or LED) as you would in darkness‚Äîthe new moon is invisible, but she is there.

Hold your crystal in your hands. Apply your oil to your wrists and breathe it in.

Sit comfortably. If you can see the night sky, gaze toward where the moon rests‚Äîhidden but present.

"In this darkness, I plant seeds of intention."

2. Reflect on What You Want to Grow

The new moon asks: What do you want to call into your life?

Close your eyes. Place your hands over your heart. Ask yourself:

  • What quality do I want to cultivate? (Peace, courage, clarity, joy?)
  • What relationship needs tending?
  • What project or dream is ready to begin?

Choose 1-3 intentions. No more. The moon grows slowly‚Äîso will your work.

3. Write Your Intentions

Write each intention as if it's already happening:

  • "I am cultivating peace in my daily life."
  • "My creative work flows with ease."
  • "I speak my truth with clarity and love."

Read them aloud. Let the words land in your body. Notice how they feel.

4. Charge Your Crystal

Hold your crystal over your written intentions. Visualize moonlight (even though she's dark) pouring into the stone.

Say: "These intentions are planted. As the moon grows, so do they."

Keep this crystal visible somewhere you'll see it daily‚Äîby your bed, on your desk, in your pocket.

"What I plant in darkness will bloom in light."

üåï Full Moon: Releasing Burdens

What You'll Need

Labradorite, Amethyst, or Selenite
Essential oil: Lavender, Clary Sage, or Cedarwood
Candle OR alternative (white or blue | LED | water bowl | visualization)
Paper and pen
Fire-safe bowl OR option to tear/bury paper

1. Bathe in Moonlight

If possible, stand or sit where the full moon can see you. Let her light touch your skin.

Hold your crystal in both hands. Apply your oil to your third eye (between brows) and heart.

Light your candle. Watch the flame dance in the moonlight.

"Under this full moon, I release what weighs me down."

2. Name What You're Releasing

The full moon illuminates everything‚Äîincluding what you no longer need.

Write down what you're ready to let go:

  • Patterns that no longer serve you
  • Relationships that drain you
  • Beliefs that limit you
  • Grief you've carried long enough

Be specific. Name it clearly so you can release it fully.

3. Release Ritual

If burning: Safely burn the paper while saying, "I release this to the moon. It is no longer mine to carry."

If tearing: Rip the paper into small pieces and either bury them or throw them into running water (stream, ocean).

If neither: Simply tear it once and place it under the moonlight overnight, then dispose of it the next day.

Watch it leave your hands. Feel the lightness that follows.

4. Cleanse and Close

Hold your crystal up to the moon (or visualize moonlight if indoors). Let it absorb the full moon's energy.

Place both hands on your heart. Say:

"I am lighter. I am clear. I honor what was and release what no longer serves. The moon holds me as I let go."

Leave your crystal in moonlight overnight if possible (windowsill works). It will cleanse and recharge.

"As the moon wanes, so does my burden. I am free."

"As I intend, in light and love."

The Aether carries it so.
